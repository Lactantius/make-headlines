How to Make Headlines

Headlines want to get your attention. That means they often evoke strong emotions.

Sometimes it's fun to try to think of ways you could rewrite a headline to change its emotional value. With this website, you can quantify your success at doing so (at least in a limited way.) Each day it gathers new headlines from various major news sources and scores them with a sentiment analysis tool using a pre-trained model. In other words, it uses an AI to predict whether a human would call the headline positive or negative.

When you input a sentence, the server scores what you wrote using the same tool, then compares it to the original and gives you a score from 0 to 200, where 0 means it has the same emotional value and 200 means it is as different as possible.

Do be aware that the computer is only asking, "Is this positive or negative, and how certain am I?" The score has nothing to do with just how positive or negative a headline is; it's only about how certain the computer is. So, for example, “The mayor is doing a pretty decent job” and “OMG THIS IS THE BEST MAYOR EVER!!!” will be scored the same; even though one is clearly much more positive than the other, the computer is equally sure they are both positive. Also, you can get high scores by writing random positive or negative scores. But that's cheating. :-(

It's all written in Python (Flask framework) and TypeScript. You can see the source here.
